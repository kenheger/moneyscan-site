Page Title (H1)
Real Estate Crowdfunding: Passive Real Estate Exposure
Introduction / Hero Section
First Principle Gain diversified real estate ownership and potential income/appreciation without the hassles of direct property management. This adds an alternative growth and income stream that can complement — but not replace — your core Wealth Engine of consistent stock-market compounding.
Why This Matters Traditional real estate requires large capital, active management, and illiquidity. Crowdfunding lowers the barrier dramatically, letting you start small while keeping most of your capital working in liquid, high-growth assets like broad-market index funds.

Key Takeaway Crowdfunding offers real estate exposure with far less capital and effort than direct ownership. Returns are not guaranteed and can vary with market cycles, but many platforms target blended income + appreciation in the mid-single to low-double digits.
Real-World Achievers
Non-accredited investors have used platforms like Fundrise and Arrived to build diversified real estate portfolios starting with small amounts. Many report steady dividend income from rentals and gradual appreciation, using these holdings as a complement to stock-market compounding rather than a replacement. Success stories often highlight consistent small contributions over years, similar to dollar-cost averaging in the Wealth Engine.
Simple Actionable Vehicles (Examples Only)
These popular platforms make passive real estate accessible. Research current offerings, fees, liquidity terms, and risks thoroughly — real estate cycles affect performance, and capital is typically locked up for years.
1.	Fundrise A diversified real estate platform offering exposure to residential, commercial, and other properties through eREITs and funds. It provides broad passive real estate allocation with very low entry barriers. • Minimum investment: $10 (taxable accounts); higher for some retirement accounts • Typical annual fees: ~1% total (0.15% advisory + 0.85% management) • Practical note: Choose income-focused or growth plans; many users automate small monthly contributions. Good for broad diversification without picking individual properties. Hold period is often 5+ years recommended.
2.	Arrived Focuses on fractional ownership in individual single-family rental properties (or funds), delivering quarterly dividends from rental income plus potential appreciation. • Minimum investment: $100 per property (or lower for some funds) • Targets: 3–5%+ dividend yields from rentals; total returns historically projected in the 6–10% range (income + appreciation) • Practical note: Build a portfolio gradually across multiple homes. Some secondary market trading windows exist for limited liquidity. Ideal if you want more direct exposure to residential rentals without landlord duties.
How to Execute Start small and diversify across properties or funds. Treat this as a portfolio diversifier (e.g., 5–20% allocation) rather than your primary wealth builder. Combine with automatic contributions from surplus created by Cash Flow Mastery or House Hacking.
Important Caveats
•	Illiquidity: Money is typically tied up for years; early exits may incur penalties or limited secondary market options.
•	Risks: Returns are not guaranteed. Real estate values and rental demand fluctuate with economic conditions, interest rates, and local markets. Fees reduce net returns.
•	No guarantees: Historical or targeted returns (e.g., 7–12% range) do not predict future performance.
•	These are general examples only. This is educational direction, not personalized advice. Review platform prospectuses, understand tax implications, and consider your risk tolerance and time horizon.
